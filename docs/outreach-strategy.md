# Outreach Strategy

## Project

Never Too Late To Fly — Dj.B. Aviation Impact Project

Purpose:
Build meaningful aviation partnerships around lifelong learning, aviation development, emergency response capability, and social/environmental impact.

---

# Recipient Types

## 1. Corporate Partner

Examples:
- aviation companies
- aerospace organizations
- CSR departments
- aviation foundations

Goal:
Create partnership opportunities.

Communication style:
- professional
- respectful
- impact-focused

Emphasize:
- aviation journey
- safety culture
- lifelong learning
- environmental responsibility
- emergency response vision

Never include in initial outreach:
- bank account information
- donation requests
- financial assistance requests

Preferred call to action:
Request a conversation or explore collaboration opportunities.

---

## 2. Aviation Mentor

Examples:
- pilots
- instructors
- aviation professionals
- experienced industry members

Goal:
Build relationships and seek guidance.

Communication style:
- personal
- humble
- authentic

Emphasize:
- learning from experience
- aviation passion
- professional development

Preferred call to action:
Ask for advice, perspective, or mentorship.

---

## 3. Individual Supporter

Examples:
- aviation enthusiasts
- private individuals
- people inspired by the journey

Goal:
Allow people to support the mission if they choose.

Communication style:
- personal
- transparent
- grateful

Financial support information:
Allowed only when the person expresses interest in helping or supporting the journey.

Never use pressure or emotional manipulation.

---

# General Rules

Always:
- Be authentic.
- Avoid exaggerated claims.
- Avoid asking for money in first contact.
- Personalize communication.
- Explain why the recipient is relevant.

Never:
- Send mass generic messages.
- Promise outcomes that are not guaranteed.
- Present the project as charity.
